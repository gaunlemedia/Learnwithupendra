Core Rule:

Singular subjects must take singular verbs, and plural subjects must take plural verbs.

Example (Singular): He likes reading novels. (He = Singular, likes = Singular verb)

Example (Plural): They like playing basketball. (They = Plural, like = Plural verb)

Tense represents the form taken by a verb to show the time of an action.

Present Tense

Subject + V1/V5 + Object

"She cooks food."

Past Tense

Subject + V2 + Object

"She cooked food."

Future Tense

Subject + will/shall + V1 + Object

"She will cook food."

Active: The focus is on the doer of the action.

Passive: The focus is on the receiver of the action (Object turns into Subject).

Active: Ram kicked the ball.

Passive: The ball was kicked by Ram.

Direct speech reports exact words spoken. Indirect speech reports the general meaning of what was said without quotation marks.

Direct: He said, "I am tired."

Indirect: He said that he was tired.
